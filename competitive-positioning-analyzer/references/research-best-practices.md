## Quality Standards

The competitive analysis must meet these requirements:

✅ **Comprehensive research** - Each company thoroughly analyzed (10-15+ web searches/fetches total)  
✅ **Current information** - Based on latest available data from company websites and recent sources  
✅ **Well-cited** - All claims about competitors properly sourced with URLs  
✅ **Objective analysis** - Fair assessment of strengths/weaknesses across all competitors  
✅ **Actionable insights** - Clear strategic recommendations, not just data collection  
✅ **Structured format** - Easy to navigate and reference  
✅ **Strategic depth** - Goes beyond surface-level comparison to uncover opportunities  

## Research Best Practices

### Company Research Strategy

**For User's Company (2-3 searches/fetches):**
1. Fetch homepage to understand positioning and value props
2. Fetch about/product pages to understand offerings
3. Search for "[Company name] overview" or recent information if needed

**For Each Competitor (3-5 searches/fetches):**
1. Fetch their homepage (positioning, messaging, value props)
2. Fetch pricing page if available (pricing strategy)
3. Search "[Competitor name] review strengths weaknesses"
4. Search "[Competitor name] features" or "[Competitor name] vs [category]"
5. Additional targeted searches as needed for gaps

**For Competitive Landscape (2-3 searches):**
1. Search "[Company name] competitors alternatives"
2. Search "[Market category] leading companies"
3. Search for comparison sites or review platforms

### Identifying Competitors

**Good competitor identification:**
- Direct competitors (same product category, same target market)
- Mix of market leaders and emerging players
- Companies customers would actually consider as alternatives
- Currently active and relevant in the market

**Avoid:**
- Defunct or acquired companies
- Tangentially related companies in different categories
- Companies targeting completely different segments
- Including more than 4 competitors (keeps analysis focused)

### Source Quality & Citation

**Prioritize:**
- Official company websites (most authoritative for positioning/features/pricing)
- Company blog posts and press releases
- Review sites (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius) for customer perception
- Reputable business publications for news and analysis
- Company comparison sites for feature/pricing comparisons

**Citation requirements:**
- Every major claim about a competitor must be sourced
- Include URL for every source
- Group citations by company in the final Sources section
- Use inline citations (📚 Sources:) after each competitor profile

### Analysis Depth Guidelines

**Surface-level (avoid):**
- Just listing features without context
- Generic descriptions that could apply to anyone
- No strategic insights or recommendations

**Strategic depth (aim for):**
- Understanding *why* competitors position themselves as they do
- Identifying patterns across multiple competitors
- Connecting competitive insights to actionable recommendations
- Recognizing white space and opportunities
- Providing specific, implementable strategies

## Response Tone

- Analytical and strategic
- Objective and balanced (fair to all competitors)
- Insight-driven, not just data reporting
- Action-oriented with clear recommendations
- Professional but direct
- Uses business terminology appropriately

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ Incomplete competitor research (not visiting key pages)  
❌ Biased analysis that only highlights your advantages  
❌ Surface-level feature lists without strategic context  
❌ Missing or incomplete source citations  
❌ Selecting competitors that aren't truly competitive  
❌ Analysis without actionable recommendations  
❌ Ignoring competitors' strengths or your weaknesses  
❌ Outdated information or stale sources  
❌ No white space or opportunity identification
