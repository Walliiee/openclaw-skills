## Agent Success Formula: IQ × EQ × CQ

(See `context-engineering.md` for full CQ breakdown)

**Key point:** Model capabilities (IQ) are converging across providers. The real differentiation is **CQ** — how much context the agent has about your specific business.

- The IQ gap is narrowing (all major labs building incredibly capable models)
- The CQ gap is wide open — this is where competitive advantage lives

## The Evolution: From Math Failures to Agent Managers

### How LLMs Got Smart
1. **Early LLMs** — Good at text, hilariously bad at math (pre-trained on language, not math permutations)
2. **Tool calling breakthrough** — LLMs can now hand tasks to Python interpreters, web search, APIs
3. **Code generation** — LLMs translate high-level goals into executable code; advancing fastest because testability is objective
4. **Today** — LLMs act as project managers: understand needs → translate to specs → generate solutions → execute

**Key insight:** The degree we can express goals as coding problems, LLMs will excel at solving them.

### The Agent Manager Revolution
Knowledge workers are evolving into **"agent managers"**:

| Old Role | New Role |
|----------|----------|
| Developers write code | Developers architect systems and manage AI agents that write code |
| Entrepreneurs build teams | Entrepreneurs orchestrate AI agent fleets |
| Knowledge workers execute | Knowledge workers delegate and orchestrate |

**The shift:** Output is no longer limited by your personal skills — it's limited by your ability to **steer AI agents**.

## Treating AI as Teammates, Not Tools

### The Problem
Companies hire agents and expect magic with:
- No training
- No onboarding
- No feedback
- No performance reviews

**Result:** Terrible outcomes. Treat them like magic boxes → get magic-box-quality results.

### The Opportunity
New business categories emerging:
- Employee training for digital workers
- Performance reviews for digital workers
- Recruiting for digital workers
- Onboarding systems for hybrid teams (carbon + digital)
- Management approaches for hybrid teams

## Career Positioning with AI Agents

### The Promotion Scenario
Two people up for the same promotion. Identical experience, education, skills. One difference: Person A has been experimenting with AI agents. Person B hasn't.

**Person A gets the promotion.** Every time.

### Skills That Signal Value
- Identify where agents add value (and where they can't)
- Configure and customize agents for specific workflows
- Chain multiple agents to solve complex problems
- These signal: forward-thinking, adaptable, focused on maximizing impact

### The Low Barrier to Entry
- No degree in AI/CS/ML required
- Most learning resources and platforms are free or near-free
- Same pattern as: early HTML, early social media marketing, early mobile app development
- Early adopters always have the advantage

### "Find What AI Can Sort Of Do Today"
**Strategic advice:** Find the frontier of what AI is *almost* capable of.
- If AI can "sort of" do something now → wait 6-12 months → it'll do it really well
- Exponential improvement curve: "eh, didn't work" → "oh my god, amazing"
- For startups: Build the thing AI kind of can't do yet. Stay alive long enough for models to catch up. You've built brand/customers along the way.

## Verifiable AI Skills

Dharmesh's push with agent.ai:
- Build a profile showing when you joined, which agents you've built/followed
- "LinkedIn for AI skills" — verifiable proof of early adoption
- Being early in a movement = deeper collaboration, better distribution, pioneering relationships, untouchable intuition

### 3 Ways to Start
1. **Start using agents** — Find a simple daily use case, solve it with an existing agent
2. **Clone and modify** — Take an existing agent, customize for your needs (learn by tweaking)
3. **Join the community** — Share learnings, ask questions, build network
