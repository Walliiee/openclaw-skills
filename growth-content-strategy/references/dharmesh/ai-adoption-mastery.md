# AI Adoption Mastery

Frameworks for systematically improving how individuals and teams adopt and use AI. Sourced from Dharmesh Shah's 60/30/10 Rule, AI as a Superpower, Keeping Up with AI, and Blank Slate Advantage.

---

## The 60/30/10 Rule

Most people use <10% of AI's potential. They find a handful of prompts that work and repeat them forever. The 60/30/10 framework fixes this:

### Three Buckets

| Bucket | % | Purpose | Example |
|--------|---|---------|---------|
| **Repetition** | 60% | Proven, reliable use cases | Summarize notes, draft emails, create outlines |
| **Iteration** | 30% | Refining *how* you ask (same task, better prompt) | "Summarize for a technical audience focusing on implementation challenges" vs generic "summarize this" |
| **Experimentation** | 10% | Pure exploration — new applications you're not sure will work | Using AI as a simulation engine, scenario planning, novel workflows |

### Implementation Steps

**Week 1 — Reality Check:**
- Track every AI interaction for one week
- Log: what you asked, which bucket it falls in, whether the result was useful or "okay"
- Most people discover 95% of usage is repetition

**Week 2+ — The Shift:**
- For ~10 daily AI interactions: aim for 6 repetition / 3 iteration / 1 experiment
- Don't stress exact numbers — ballpark is fine

**Daily Experimentation Habit:**
- Before opening your usual apps for ANY task, type it into ChatGPT first
- Expect most experiments to fail — the ones that work become part of your toolkit
- Keep an experiment log: What I tried → Did it work? → How to improve?

**Pro tip:** If an experiment fails today, set a reminder to retry in 3-6 months. AI capabilities evolve rapidly.

### AI Intuition (The Outcome)

After consistent 60/30/10 practice, you develop "AI intuition":
- "This writing task is perfect for AI — structured, clear examples available"
- "This strategic decision needs human judgment, but AI can help think through options"
- "Too much nuanced context here — I'll handle this myself"

**The shift:** From "I have a problem, maybe AI can help" → "I have work to do, how should AI be involved from the beginning?"

## You^AI — The Power Formula

**Core concept:** The right mental framework is **You^AI** — AI as an exponential amplifier, not a replacement.

- You're not less valuable because you use spell check instead of a dictionary
- You're not less valuable because you use Google instead of memorizing encyclopedias
- AI amplifies capabilities — but only if you actually use it, learn it, experiment with it

**The PhD Intern analogy:** Pretend you have access to an intern with a PhD in everything. Before spending time on any task, give it a crack at solving the problem first. You'll be surprised how often it produces something helpful you wouldn't have expected.

**Daily rule:** Every day, you should be in ChatGPT (or Claude, Gemini, etc.). No exceptions. Regardless of job or role.

## The Blank Slate Advantage

**Origin:** Dharmesh's 14-year-old son uses AI for fantasy world-building with 2,000-word prompts, treating AI as a "simulation engine." He tries things adults wouldn't because he doesn't know the limitations.

**Key insight:** "Why wouldn't it be able to do that? It's supposed to be able to do everything."

### Adults vs Kids
| Adults | Kids |
|--------|------|
| "What's the answer to X?" | "What would happen if X? What about Y? What if I combined X and Y?" |
| Use AI to retrieve answers | Use AI to explore possibilities |
| Limited by knowledge of limitations | Unlimited by assumption of capability |

### Business Application — Simulation Thinking
1. Feed AI a detailed knowledge base (products, strategy, constraints, customers)
2. Ask it to simulate scenarios: "What if we launched product X?" / "What if we changed pricing?"
3. Ask for feedback from multiple perspectives: "What would our VP Sales, Head of Product, a customer, and a VC each say about this?"

**AI becomes a simulation engine** — not one perspective, but multiple angles you might not have considered. Not as good as talking to real people, but much faster for refining ideas before bringing them to humans.

## Keeping Up with AI (Without Drowning)

### The 3-Part Strategy

**1. Use AI Daily — Don't Just Study It**
- Hands-on experience teaches more than 100 research papers
- Develops intuitive sense of what's possible
- Start with: AI notetaker in meetings, summarize long emails, research with agents

**2. Schedule Quarterly AI Opportunity Reviews**
- List your top 3 growth-hindering challenges
- Check if anything in AI landscape now addresses them
- Run small experiments → scale what works
- Make this a regular, scheduled event

**3. Try New Tools with Specific Problems**
- Don't play aimlessly — approach with a concrete problem
- Problem-first evaluation: gives you a way to measure usefulness
- Build a mental library of "which tools excel at which problems"

### The Big Mistake to Avoid
- Don't decide "AI can't do X" and forget about it
- Keep a running "not yet, but soon" list
- Review it quarterly — the answer changes faster than you expect

## Frameworks for Workshops/Presentations

When teaching AI adoption to teams, use these in sequence:

1. **Reality Check** (60/30/10 Week 1) — Show them how repetitive their current usage is
2. **You^AI framing** — Reframe from "AI vs me" to "AI amplifies me"
3. **PhD Intern analogy** — Make it tangible and non-threatening
4. **Blank Slate exercise** — Have them try something they assume AI can't do
5. **Experiment Log** — Give them a tracking structure
6. **Quarterly review cadence** — Institutionalize the practice
