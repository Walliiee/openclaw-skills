# Content Creation Strategy

Frameworks for creating content that stands out in the AI era. Sourced from Dharmesh Shah's Reshaping Content Creation.

---

## The Content Paradox

Two opposing arguments — both correct simultaneously:

1. **"Good content rises to the top"** — Quality has always been the differentiator ✅
2. **"We're drowning in AI-generated content"** — Content is 100x easier to create → at least 100x more content ✅

**The paradox:** Even if your content is good, you're competing against an exponentially larger pool — including fully AI creators. Yet there's never been a more important time to be a skilled content creator. Companies pay significant sums for creators who cut through the noise.

**Question:** Not whether content creation is valuable (it is, more than ever) — but what *type* of content survives the AI flood.

## The Human Experience Moat

**Core strategy:** Focus on creating content that AI **fundamentally cannot generate**.

### What AI Can't Replicate
- Your personal journey
- Unique insights from years in an industry
- Specific failures and lessons learned
- Genuine reactions to events as they unfold
- Lived experience and authentic perspective

### The Shift (Especially on LinkedIn)

| **Then (Best Practice)** | **Now (Best Practice)** |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Keep profile updated with credentials — school, jobs, achievements, certifications | Post regularly with actual ideas, thoughts, reactions, projects, insights from your work |
| **SHOW WHAT YOU'VE DONE** | **SHARE WHAT YOU THINK** |

### Why This Works
- Establishes context so right people/opportunities (and AI agents) can connect dots around your expertise
- Authenticity > commercialization
- Generic corporate speak = what AI does best → don't compete there
- Genuine generosity compounds: help elevate others, provide honest feedback, value others' time

## Practical Content Strategy

### What to Create
- **Document real experiences** — What actually happened, not theoretical takes
- **Share actual thoughts on industry developments** — Your genuine reaction, not a summary
- **Tell failure stories + lessons learned** — Most valuable because most vulnerable
- **React authentically to events in your field** — Timeliness + perspective

### What NOT to Create
- Generic summaries AI can produce
- Corporate-speak listicles
- Content without a personal angle or unique insight
- "10 tips for X" without lived experience behind them

### The Content Audit Question
For every piece of content, ask: **"Could AI generate this without my lived experience?"**
- If yes → don't bother, it won't stand out
- If no → that's your moat, lean into it

## LinkedIn as the Platform

Dharmesh considers LinkedIn one of the most **underrated career hacks** available:
- World's best platform for connecting professional dots
- Those who stand out share unique human perspective
- Bonus: come from genuine generosity — help elevate others

### Implementation
1. **Braindump content** — Ask: "What unique value do I bring based on personal life experiences?"
2. **Post regularly** — Consistency > perfection
3. **Lead with insight** — Not credentials, not company news — your actual thinking
4. **Engage authentically** — Comment with substance, not just "Great post!"
