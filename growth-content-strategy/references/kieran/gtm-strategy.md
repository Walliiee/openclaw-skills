# GTM Strategy & AI Marketing Playbook

Frameworks for reshaping go-to-market with AI — from growing influence and demand to converting revenue. Includes the 5 new marketing roles for the AI era, intent engine architecture, micro-audience campaigns, and the marketing dashboard framework.

## 3 GTM Trends That Matter

### 1. Influence > Clicks
- 6-8 out of 10 searches result in zero clicks (AI mode will accelerate this)
- Influence happens off-site: Share of Voice in AI engines, YouTube, podcasts, newsletters
- People default to humans over brands — creator-led content drives more demand than branded content
- Content that moves a market will carry a face

### 2. AEO > SEO
- By 2028, LLMs capture 75% of search traffic share (SEMRush)
- Entity > keyword — models care about who/what you are and whether you're referenced across the web
- HubSpot results: most visible brand by SoV, demand from AI engines up 1850%, citations up 433%

### 3. Value > Volume
- Fewer visits → extract more value per visit
- AI engine traffic converts at 3x rate of normal organic traffic
- Key metric: **MRR per Human Engagement** — is the value of every sales touch trending up?
- Value formula: Context + Sales Agents + AI-Enabled Sellers

## AI Marketing Playbook

### How to Grow Demand & Influence

**AI Search (AEO):**
- Grow share of voice in LLMs for relevant queries
- Many micro-niche page versions, third-party citations, pages optimized for LLMs
- Tools: xFunnel, UserBear.ai, LLMs for query mapping

**Short-form Content:**
- Master short-form across text and video
- 65% of B2B buyers prefer short-form content
- Tools: LLM as content assistant, VEO3, Captions Mirage

**Code-Powered Experiences:**
- Turn static content into interactive experiences (free apps, tools, agents)
- 70% of B2B buyers prefer self-serve tools or interactive experiences
- Tools: Lovable, Claude Code, Agent.ai

### How to Convert Demand

**Intent Engine:**
- Move from reactive (waiting on clicks) to proactive (meeting buyers where they are)
- Capture external + internal intent signals mapped to ICP
- Fine-tune AI experiences to converting intent signals
- 93% of B2B marketers report improved conversion rates with intent data

External intent signals:
- Job postings & hiring activity
- Executive LinkedIn job changes
- Tech stack / firmographic changes
- Browsing/keyword spikes on G2, review sites
- Search signals on StackOverflow, Hacker News

**AI Sales Prospecting:**
- AI personalized outbound/inbound emails
- Automated call scripts
- Reps spend only 28-33% of time selling — AI reclaims the rest
- Tools: HubSpot, intent engine, HeyGen (personalized video)

**Micro-Audience Campaigns:**
- Replace broad segments with hyper-targeted campaigns per micro-audience
- Split ICP into micro-audiences using intent engine
- Cluster and score each audience on intent signals
- Auto-generate campaign briefs: Signal → Pain → Hook → Campaign
- Relevant messaging increases CTRs by up to 209%, conversions by 3-5x

## 5 New Marketing Roles for AI

### 1. AI Engineer
- Systems thinker who integrates AI across marketing workflows
- Skills: Replit/Lovable, n8n/Zapier, ChatGPT/Claude, systems thinking
- Biggest blocker for AI adoption = inability to go deep with automation

### 2. AI Trainer
- Trains and iterates on deployed agents (sales, onboarding, upsell)
- Skills: Prompt design, feedback loops, fine-tuning for specific use cases
- Badly trained agents wreck trust across customer journey

### 3. AEO Specialist (AI Engine Optimization)
- Evolution of SEO team — optimizes for AI engine visibility
- Skills: How AI engines surface answers, content structure, citation strategies
- "Page 1 of Google" → "default answer in ChatGPT"

### 4. Creator-Led Content Manager
- Builds creator networks (internal/external) for ICP influence
- Skills: Spot niche voices, hire/partner, AI production tools, interlink creators
- People follow people, not brands — even in B2B

### 5. Intent Signal Strategist
- Combines GTM tools, product analytics, social listening, external scraping
- Uses AI to stitch intent signals into actionable intelligence
- Seen 500%+ increases in conversion rates with intent + AI personalization

## Marketing Dashboard Framework

Three columns that tell the full story of marketing:

### Column 1: Grow Influence (hardest to defend, most important)
- **LLM Share of Voice** — does your brand show up when someone asks the AI?
- **Branded Traffic** — organic/direct visits from people searching your name
- **Aided & Unaided Awareness** — "Have you heard of X?" vs "Name a CRM"
- **Total Impressions by Country** — reach across key markets

### Column 2: Grow Demand (marketers know how to defend this)
- **Paid Advertising Leads** — still works, CPC up 50-100% since ChatGPT
- **SEO Leads** — informational declining, transactional holding/growing
- **Meetings Booked** — inbound vs outbound, AI prospecting makes outbound viable
- **Website Chat** — volume + revenue together (chat is now a conversion channel)

### Column 3: Grow Revenue (didn't exist on marketing dashboards 2 years ago)
- **Deal Velocity** — AI compresses time to close (discovery, follow-ups, objection surfacing)
- **Sales Efficiency** — as touches-to-close ↓, MRR per human touch ↑
- **Rep Time Reclaimed** — AI prospecting/research/prep gives sellers time back
- **Closed-Won Rate** — better AI discovery = better qualification = cleaner pipeline

## AI Sales Agents (HubSpot learnings)

### Website Agent (Salesbot)
- Handles 80% of website chat, increased MRR per human engagement by 2.3x
- Trained on sales framework → qualify demand, identify intent signals
- Not just answering questions — deciphering intent to buy

### AI-SDR (Prospecting)
- Email-only: +30% increase in meetings booked
- Multi-touch sequences: +10% increase in outcomes
- Key learnings:
  - Rep Tone > Marketing Tone (co-create with reps)
  - Prompts as modular blocks (intent → tone → message → evaluation)
  - Context & data quality matter more than prompt skill
  - Treat copy like a product (weekly A/B testing)
  - Training managers to coach AI usage = critical

### Discovery Agent (Experimental)
- AI assistant handles first conversation post-booking
- Answers questions, gathers context, passes to rep
- Goal: improve deal velocity + closed-won rates

## Context Layer (The 5% vs 95% Difference)

95% of AI projects fail in enterprise despite $30-40B in spending (MIT study). Core reason: requires manual context input every time.

A great context layer:
- Transforms raw signals (internal + external, structured + unstructured) into AI insights
- Gets smarter with usage
- Makes it easy to reuse context across agents and GTM employees
- The user doesn't start from scratch every time
